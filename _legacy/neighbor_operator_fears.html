The question everyone asks first

Is this an MLM?

It is a fair question. The internet is full of "business in a box" pitches that turn out to be recruiting schemes with monthly fees. Here is how to tell the difference.

MLMs make money when you recruit.
You make money when you walk dogs.

There is no downline. There is no commission on other operators. There is no pressure to recruit your friends. The platform earns its $149 a month from your subscription — and that is the only way it earns money from you, ever.

You are a business owner. You hire who you need, when you need them. A manager. Three walkers. Whatever the route demands. The platform is the operating system. The business is yours.

The honest version of this opportunity: the platform gives you tools, a protected territory, and a tailwind. It does not give you customers. You go get those. Every successful operator wearing their t-shirt at the dog park, leaving flyers at the vet, showing up at dog shows — that is the job. The platform makes the business run. You make it grow.

MLM

  • Income depends on recruiting others
  • Monthly fee regardless of your activity

  • $149/month — the software that runs your business

  • Territory is a sellable asset you own
  • Your customer database transfers with the sale
