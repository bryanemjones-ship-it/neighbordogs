Based on ChatGPT research + Claude analysis of underserved app
markets (apps with 3.0-3.8 stars, 10K-100K downloads = "users
need it but hate it").

   Why: Pickleball booming, facility managers non-technical,
   switching costs low, most using spreadsheets/phone calls.
   Weakness: Bigger players (CourtReserve) could notice.
   Pricing: $49-99/month per facility.

2. Shift Scheduling for Small Businesses
   Why: WhenIWork/Deputy charge $4-8/user/month, over-engineered
   for an 8-person coffee shop. Dead-simple $29/mo flat rate
   would spread by word of mouth. Massive market, daily usage
   means high retention.
   Weakness: Competitive, but incumbents are hated.
   Overlaps with: SchedSolvee core engine.

3. Church Management
   Why: 300K+ churches in US. Planning Center charges $50-200/mo,
   small churches can't justify it. They need: member directory,
   volunteer scheduling, giving/donations, event calendar.
   Strength: Churches talk to each other — one sale becomes five
   through denominational networks.
   Weakness: Long sales cycles, relationship-driven.

ADU BUILDFLOW — COMPETITIVE RESEARCH
================================================================

Confirmed competitive gap: No tool does full pipeline
(feasibility → permit → build → inspect) in one ADU-specific
product. Market is fragmented.

Existing tools (none are direct competitors):
- Canibuild: $195/mo, feasibility/3D viz only. Sales tool.
- Symbium Build: Free, computational law for zoning. B2G model.
- Opoplan/Open ADU: Lot-level feasibility only.
- Buildertrend: Generic construction PM, not ADU-specific.
- PermitFlow: AI permit management, all construction types.

Positioning: "Canibuild tells you IF you can build. BuildFlow
manages the entire project AFTER you say yes, and includes
feasibility as part of a unified pipeline."

Target: ADU design-build firms, prefab companies, consultants.
Market: 20K+ ADUs permitted annually in CA alone.
Price: $149-199/month (highest in AppStack portfolio).
