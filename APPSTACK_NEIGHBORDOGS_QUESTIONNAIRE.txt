APPSTACK NEIGHBORDOGS — PRODUCT INTAKE QUESTIONNAIRE
Completed: 2026-04-17
Product: NeighborDogs (rebuild on AppStack factory stack)
Prior version: Single-HTML-file app with PHP API, currently live,
               one paying owner at $149/month
Target stack: Next.js + Supabase + Stripe + Resend + Twilio +
              Vercel + Cloudflare + i18n (6 languages) + Liquid Glass

TERMINOLOGY LOCKED FOR THIS PRODUCT:
- Owner = the person who pays AppStack $149/month for a territory.
  Runs the business. Owns the territory as a sellable asset.
- Team member = works under an owner, scoped to the owner's territory.
- Customer = the person who wants their dog walked. Pays the owner.
- AppStack admin = Bryan. Platform-level only.

==================================================
PART 1 — THE PROBLEM
==================================================

1.1 What problem does this app solve in one sentence?

Running a solo dog walking business — scheduling, GPS-tracked
walks, photo reports, payments, customers — without hiring a
manager or paying franchise fees.

1.2 Who has this problem?

A solo owner running a dog walking business in their own
neighborhood. Typically a retiree, a career-changer, or someone
building a side business. Low-to-moderate tech sophistication.
Uses a phone in the field, a laptop at home. Not a developer.
Wants the business to run itself between walks.

1.3 What does the user currently do to solve this problem?

Spreadsheets, texts, paper notes, Venmo. Or they pay for Time
To Pet ($50+/month) but get no territory protection and no
resale value. Or they join Rover/Wag and give up 20-40% to the
marketplace.

1.4 What is the cost of NOT solving this problem?

Lost walks from scheduling mistakes. No proof of service when
a customer disputes. No way to sell the business when they're
done. Constant phone-tag with customers. Burnout.

1.5 What is ONE thing the user will do in the app more than
    anything else?

Complete a walk — arrive, start GPS tracking, finish, upload
photos, send the report card.

1.6 What does success look like after using the app?

A route of recurring customers who book themselves, pay
automatically, and receive a GPS-tracked report with photos
after every walk. A territory that's theirs. An asset they
can sell.

1.7 Compliance, legal, regulatory constraints?

General liability insurance required before territory activates.
Liability waiver signed at customer registration (refined later
in Part 4 — signed at meet and greet). No employee/contractor
classification issues — owner is the independent business
owner, AppStack is the platform.

==================================================
PART 2 — THE USER
==================================================

2.1 Is there one type of user or multiple?

Four distinct user types:
- Owner (territory owner — the dog walker running the business)
- Team member (works under an owner, scoped to that territory)
- Customer (dog owner booking walks)
- AppStack admin (Bryan — platform-level oversight, territory
  approvals, resale marketplace)

2.2 For each user type: primary job to be done?

- Owner: complete walks, manage customers, get paid, grow the
  route
- Team member: complete walks assigned to them
- Customer: book walks, see proof of service, pay
- AppStack admin: approve territories, manage resales, monitor
  platform health

2.3 For each user type: what do they NOT need to see or do?

- Owner: does not see other owners' territories, customers, or
  revenue
- Team member: does not see billing, territory purchase, or
  resale marketplace
- Customer: does not see owner business data, other customers,
  or any admin function
- AppStack admin: does not need to perform walks or manage
  individual customers

2.4 Are users individuals or do they belong to
    organizations/teams?

Owners are individuals who own a territory. A territory is the
team. Team members belong to a territory. Customers are
individuals attached to a territory.

2.5 Can a user belong to more than one team/organization?

An owner can own multiple territories. A team member can be on
multiple territories simultaneously. A customer belongs to one
territory (the one serving their address).

2.6 What is the minimum viable user?

A solo owner with one territory and no team members, walking
their own customers. No team management, no resale, no
multi-territory dashboard needed to get value.

2.7 What does the user's typical session look like?

- Owner: multiple times per day. Morning route planning, walk
  execution, end-of-day review. In the field on a phone.
- Customer: weekly. Books a walk, gets a report, views gallery.
- AppStack admin: weekly. Territory approvals, resale contracts,
  platform monitoring.

2.8 Device and browser?

- Owner: phone in the field, laptop at home. Mobile-first
  required. Modern Chrome/Safari.
- Customer: phone primarily.
- AppStack admin: desktop.

2.9 Is the app used in the field or at a desk?

Field. The owner is outdoors with a dog on a leash, in variable
weather, often with one hand. GPS tracking runs in the active
foreground session. Bad connectivity must degrade gracefully.

==================================================
PART 3 — THE DATA
==================================================

3.1 What is the central entity?

The walk. Everything else (customers, dogs, territories, report
cards, photos, payments) exists in service of the walk.

3.2 What data does the walk contain?

- Walk ID
- Territory ID
- Owner ID
- Performer ID (owner or team member who performed it)
- Customer ID
- Dog(s) on the walk
- Walk type (20 min / 30 min / 60 min / emergency / buddy)
- Scheduled date and time
- Scheduled duration
- Actual start time
- Actual end time
- Actual duration
- GPS route (array of lat/lng points with timestamps)
- Distance walked
- Max/avg speed
- Pee status
- Poop status
- Walk notes
- Photos (up to 2)
- Weather on walk date (forecast captured at booking)
- Status (scheduled / en route / in progress / completed /
  attempted / cancelled / refunded)
- Payment status (unpaid / paid / refunded)
- Payment method (Stripe — all walk payments via Stripe per
  Part 17 scope decision)
- Price charged
- On-my-way SMS sent timestamp
- Access problem flag
- Created at / updated at

3.3 Required vs. optional?

Required: walk ID, territory ID, owner ID, customer ID, dog(s),
walk type, scheduled date/time, status, payment status, price.

Optional: everything captured during/after the walk — GPS
route, actual times, pee/poop, notes, photos, weather.

3.4 Can one user have many walks? How many is typical?

An owner performs many walks. Target density: 5-15 walks per
day at maturity. A customer may have many walks — a weekly
recurring customer has ~50/year. Over a territory lifetime
(3-5 years before resale), an owner could accumulate
5,000-15,000 walks.

3.5 Does the entity have a lifecycle? Define every state.

- Scheduled — booked, not yet started
- En route — walker started "On My Way"
- In progress — GPS tracking active
- Completed — walk finished, report card sent
- Attempted — walker arrived, no access, no walk performed
- Cancelled — cancelled before start
- Refunded — completed or attempted walk refunded after the fact

3.6 What events cause state changes?

- Scheduled → En route: walker taps "On My Way"
- En route → In progress: walker taps "Start Walk" (GPS begins)
- In progress → Completed: walker taps "End Walk" and submits
  report card
- Scheduled/En route → Attempted: walker taps "No Access"
- Scheduled → Cancelled: owner or customer cancels before start
- Completed/Attempted → Refunded: owner or admin issues refund

3.7 Deadlines or time windows?

- Emergency walks have a same-day time window (must be
  completed today)
- Report card must be submitted before the walk can be marked
  Completed
- "On My Way" SMS should fire before walk start time (customer
  expectation, not a hard deadline)

3.8 Secondary entities attached to the walk?

- Customer (owner info, address, access notes)
- Dog(s) — name, breed, notes, behavioral flags.
  NO MEDICATIONS FIELD (per Part 3 correction — AppStack does
  not store dog medication information under any circumstances)
- Territory (the geographic container)
- Report card (pee/poop/notes/photos — 1:1 with walk)
- GPS route (points — 1:many with walk)
- Photos (1:many with walk, also flow into customer gallery)
- Payment record (1:1 with walk, Stripe)
- Refund record (0 or 1 per walk)

3.9 What data comes from outside the app?

- Geocoding (address → lat/lng) from a geocoding API
- Weather forecast from Open-Meteo
- SMS delivery status from Twilio
- Stripe webhook events (subscription, walk payment, refund,
  resale)
- Supabase magic link delivery

3.10 What data must be preserved even after the user leaves?

- All completed walks (proof of service, resale asset)
- All report cards and photos (customer history)
- All payment records (tax, dispute resolution)
- Territory history (for resale marketplace provenance)
- Customer database (transfers with territory on resale)

3.11 What data must NEVER be deleted?

- Payment records — tax and dispute resolution
- Liability waiver signatures — legal defense if a dog injury
  claim arises
- Territory resale contracts — non-compete enforcement,
  ownership chain

PART 4 — PLATFORM RULES
==================================================

4.1 Does the app depend on rules or policies set by a
    third-party platform?

Partially. Not a booking platform like Airbnb. But the app
depends on:
- Stripe rules (payment processing, refund windows, chargeback
  timelines, Stripe Connect requirements for walk payments)
- Twilio rules (SMS character limits, opt-out compliance,
  A2P 10DLC registration)
- Open-Meteo rate limits and data freshness
- Insurance carrier requirements for owner general liability
  policies
- State/local rules on dog walking as a business activity
  (varies, mostly unregulated)

4.2 List every rule that affects the app's logic.

- Stripe: 120-day chargeback window on card payments
- Stripe Connect: owner onboarding requirements, identity
  verification, payout scheduling
- Twilio: 160 character SMS limit (Latin scripts); A2P 10DLC
  registration required for business SMS in US
- Twilio: opt-out keywords (STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE) must be honored
  automatically
- Insurance: COI must be on file and unexpired before territory
  activates
- Insurance: policy must explicitly cover team members operating
  under the owner's business (per Part 14 addition)
- Insurance: $1M per occurrence minimum recommended (not
  enforced by law, enforced by AppStack policy)
- Liability waiver: must be signed by customer at meet and
  greet before first walk
- Geocoding: service radius enforced server-side (1.5 mile
  default in NeighborDogs today)

4.3 How often do these rules change? Who monitors?

Platform/app health function required (same pattern as HostClaim
admin panel). Monitors third-party rule changes, integration
status, and policy-relevant alerts across the app. This is a
required module at the factory level.

- Stripe: rare, announced with notice. Changes surfaced via
  the platform health panel.
- Twilio: A2P 10DLC rules have changed frequently — monitored
  via the platform health panel.
- Insurance: carrier-specific, owner's responsibility to keep
  current. App tracks COI status and expiry.
- AppStack-set rules (service radius, COI minimums): set by
  Bryan, changed by Bryan, logged to platform health.

4.4 Rules that differ by geography, platform tier, or user
    type?

- Geography: SMS compliance differs by country. A2P 10DLC is
  US-specific. EU has GDPR implications for customer data.
  Canada has CASL for email.
- User type: owners have insurance requirements, team members
  don't, customers don't.
- Platform tier: not applicable — $149/month flat for all
  owners.

4.5 Rules the app must enforce on behalf of the platform?

- Liability waiver must be signed at meet and greet before
  customer can book walks
- COI must be on file and current before territory activates
- Owner service radius enforced on customer registration
  (prevents out-of-area signups)
- Stripe subscription must be current for territory to remain
  active
- SMS opt-outs must be honored across all future messages

4.6 Information presented that could be incorrect due to
    policy changes?

- Weather forecast (external data, could be stale or wrong)
- GPS accuracy varies by device
- Territory home count estimates are approximations
- Insurance COI status is only as current as the last upload —
  expired policies are a risk

Users warned when COI is within 30 days of expiry and when it
has expired.

PART 5 — AUTHENTICATION AND ACCESS
==================================================

5.1 How do users sign up?

Magic link across all user types (Supabase magic link).

- Owners: Supabase magic link (passwordless email)
- Team members: invited by owner via email, accept with magic
  link
- Customers: self-registration at owner's customer app URL,
  magic link
- AppStack admin: Bryan's account, magic link

One auth pattern across the whole app. Customer flow matches
owner flow — simpler, no password management.

5.2 Is email verification required before first login?

Magic link inherently verifies email. No separate verification
step needed.

5.3 Is there a trial period? Credit card at trial start?

- Owners: no trial. Territory purchase is a Stripe subscription
  from day one. COI upload gates activation, not payment.
- Customers: no subscription. Pay per walk via Stripe.

5.4 What happens when a subscription expires?

30-day grace period with staged reminders:
- Day 1: payment failed email
- Day 7: reminder, territory still active
- Day 14: warning, 16 days until deactivation
- Day 21: urgent, 9 days until deactivation
- Day 28: final notice, 2 days
- Day 30: territory deactivates

Deactivated territory:
- Cannot accept new customer registrations
- Cannot book new walks
- Existing walks in progress complete normally
- Customer database preserved during lapse, restored on
  reactivation
- Team member access suspends with territory

Rationale for 30 days (not 60): covers card replacement cycle,
short vacation, or holiday. Most SaaS uses 7-14 days. 30 is
already generous for $149/month.

5.5 Can accounts be deleted? Archived? Difference?

- Owner account: can be deleted (GDPR) or archived (territory
  sold through resale marketplace)
- Deleted = data purged per retention policy
- Archived = data retained, account inactive, territory either
  sold or released back to marketplace
- Team member: removed by owner, no account deletion (user
  record remains)
- Customer: can be deleted on request, archived by default when
  inactive

5.6 If a user deletes their account, what happens to data?

- Owner deletes: territory released, customer database
  transferred to new owner if territory is sold; if released
  back to marketplace, customers notified and given option to
  re-register with new owner
- Customer deletes: personal data purged, walk history
  anonymized but retained for owner's tax/dispute records
- Retention period for purged data: 30 days soft delete, then
  hard delete
- Payment records and liability waivers: retained beyond
  account deletion for legal reasons (see 3.11)

5.7 Admin users — what can AppStack admin (Bryan) see?

AppStack is the platform, not the service provider. Seeing
customer data creates dispute exposure. Owners are independent.
Their customers are not AppStack's customers.

AppStack admin SEES only platform-level data:
- Territory status (active / lapsed / deactivated)
- Subscription payment status (current / past due / lapsed)
- COI status (current / expiring within 30 days / expired)
- Territory resale listings and completed transactions
- Platform-wide metrics (active territory count, MRR, churn)
- Integration health (Stripe webhooks, Twilio delivery,
  Supabase uptime)

AppStack admin DOES NOT SEE:
- Customer personal data
- Customer payment details
- Walk report cards, photos, or GPS routes
- Owner-customer communications
- Individual walk records

Consequence: customer-level refunds (customer disputing a walk
charge) are handled by the owner, not AppStack. AppStack only
issues territory subscription refunds (owner-to-AppStack).

5.8 SSO for enterprise customers?

No. Not in v1. Not in any foreseeable version — user is an
independent owner, not an enterprise.

PART 6 — TEAMS AND PERMISSIONS
==================================================

6.1 Is the app solo-user only, or do teams exist?

Teams exist. An owner can work solo (most common at start) or
invite team members.

6.2 Is team membership required or optional?

Optional. An owner can run their territory solo forever. Team
membership is a growth feature.

6.3 Roles within a team?

Two roles:

Owner:
- Everything: manage customers, schedule walks, perform walks,
  view all data, manage billing, upload COI, list territory for
  resale, invite/remove team members

Team member:
- Perform walks assigned to them
- View their assigned walks and the customer/dog info needed to
  perform them
- Submit report cards and photos
- Mark walks as attempted if no access
- Capture customer waivers at meet and greet (per Part 12.3)
- Cannot: see billing, see territory financials, manage
  customers, invite other team members, list territory for
  resale, upload COI, access admin panel

6.4 Who can invite members? Who can remove them?

Only the owner. Team members cannot invite or remove other team
members.

6.5 Seat limit?

No plan tiers. All territories are $149/month flat. Unlimited
team members per territory.

Open question: does unlimited team members create abuse risk?
Keeping for v1 per existing NeighborDogs behavior. Revisit if
abuse emerges.

6.6 What happens to team data when the owner cancels?

Non-compete clause extends to team members to close the end-
around-sale loophole.

- Subscription lapse → territory deactivates → team members
  lose access
- Team member user records retained (may join another territory
  later)
- Walk history performed by team members stays attached to the
  territory (resale asset)
- If territory is sold, team members do NOT transfer — new
  owner must re-invite

End-around-sale prevention:
- A team member CAN become an owner — but only through the
  AppStack resale marketplace (10% fee preserved, customer
  database transferred cleanly, non-compete travels)
- When an owner cancels, territory enters 90-day protected
  window
- During 90 days, territory listed in resale marketplace at
  system-calculated floor price (2x trailing annual AppStack
  fees = $3,576)
- Team members who worked that territory in last 12 months get
  first right of refusal during the 90 days
- If no purchase in 90 days, territory releases back to general
  marketplace
- Team members blocked from purchasing any NEW overlapping
  territory (bypassing resale) for 12 months after owner's
  cancellation

Legal note: 12-month non-compete on independent contractors
varies by state. North Carolina allows reasonable non-competes
with consideration. Geographic scope tied to the actual
territory is defensible. Lawyer review required before first
team member signs. Captured as factory-level legal to-do.

6.7 Can a team member be on multiple teams?

Yes, simultaneously. No restriction. Team member on Territory A
and Territory B is free to do so. Non-compete in 6.6 still
applies independently per territory — being on multiple
territories doesn't change the end-around-sale block, each
territory's 12-month clock runs independently.

6.8 What does a team member see on first login?

Simplified dashboard:
- Walks assigned to them today
- Walks assigned to them this week
- Their walk history
- Basic profile (name, photo, contact info visible to owner)

Does NOT see:
- Other team members' walks
- Territory financials
- Customer contact info beyond what's needed for the walk
- Any admin or billing UI

==================================================
PART 7 — MONETIZATION
==================================================

7.1 Revenue model?

Two revenue streams:

1. Subscription — $149/month per territory, flat, paid by
   owner to AppStack
2. Marketplace fee — 10% of resale price when a territory is
   sold through the AppStack marketplace

No percentage of owner walk revenue. No franchise fee. No
setup fee.

Economics note: 10% marketplace fee is low compared to
traditional franchise (4-8% ongoing royalty forever). AppStack's
take-ratio to owner's revenue over a 4-year territory lifecycle:
~6%. Raise to 12-15% possible after 50+ owners exist and
platform value is obvious — not before. Early adopters keep 10%.

7.2 Subscription plan tiers?

One tier. $149/month. Includes:
- Territory exclusivity
- Full platform access (customer management, scheduling, GPS,
  reports, photos, team management, resale marketplace listing)
- Unlimited customers
- Unlimited team members
- Unlimited walks
- All features — no paywall inside the app

7.3 Pay-per-use?

Not applicable to owner. Owner pays flat $149/month regardless
of walk volume.

Customers pay the owner per walk. All walk payments processed
via Stripe (see Part 17 scope decision). AppStack takes nothing
from customer-owner walk payments.

7.4 Free tier?

No. Territory purchase is the commitment. COI upload gates
activation, not payment.

7.5 Plan limits mid-task?

Not applicable — no usage limits. Only hard gate is territory
boundary (customer at non-qualifying address cannot register).

7.6 Annual billing discount?

Yes. Annual option at launch: $1,490/year (saves $298,
equivalent to 2 months free). Reduces churn, smooths cash
flow. Available from day one.

7.7 Enterprise plans?

No. A multi-territory owner just buys multiple territories at
$149 each.

7.8 Features gated behind plans?

Nothing. One tier. All features unlocked on payment + COI
activation.

7.9 Pay-per-item alongside subscription?

Not applicable. Territory is the unit.

7.10 Statement descriptor on credit card statement?

NEIGHBORDOGS (Stripe descriptor limit 22 characters).
Customer sees the brand, not the parent LLC.

7.11 Refunds?

Owner refunds (AppStack to owner):
- First 30 days: full refund on request, no questions asked
- After 30 days: no refund on monthly subscription
- If COI lapses and territory deactivates: no refund for the
  period deactivated
- Annual plan: prorated refund in first 30 days, otherwise no
  refund

Customer refunds (owner to customer) — owner-initiated:
- Owner opens any completed, attempted, or cancelled walk
- Taps "Issue Refund"
- Chooses: full refund or partial refund (with amount)
- Processed via Stripe (all walk payments are Stripe per Part 17)
- Customer sees refund on their card in 5-10 business days
- Walk status updates to "Refunded"
- Customer notified automatically
- AppStack is NOT involved in the refund decision

Resale marketplace: once a resale transaction completes,
AppStack's 10% fee is non-refundable. Owner-to-owner disputes
are between the parties.

8.1 Events that trigger an email?

Owner emails:
- Magic link login
- Territory purchase confirmation
- COI uploaded / approved / expiring in 30 days / expired
- Subscription payment succeeded / failed / past due / final
  notice
- Team member invite accepted
- Customer registered in their territory
- Resale listing created / offer received / sale completed
- Monthly summary (walks completed, revenue, new customers)

Team member emails:
- Team invite (with magic link)
- Removed from team
- Walk assigned

Customer emails:
- Magic link login
- Meet and greet confirmed
- Walk booking confirmed
- Walk report card ready (with link to report)
- Refund issued
- Owner cancelled a scheduled walk

AppStack admin emails (Bryan):
- New territory purchase (requires approval)
- New COI uploaded (requires approval)
- Resale transaction completed
- Platform health alerts (Stripe webhook failures, Twilio

8.2 Events that trigger an SMS?

Customer SMS:
- "On My Way" — walker starts trip to customer's home
- Access problem — walker arrived, no access
- In-walk photo — walker snaps a photo during the walk, SMS
  sends with link to view (new feature in rebuild)

Owner SMS:
- Emergency walk booking request
- Team member marked a walk attempted (no access)

No SMS for marketing. No SMS for AppStack administrative events
to owner (those go to email).

8.3 Events that trigger in-app notifications?

Owner in-app:
- New customer registration pending meet and greet
- Emergency walk request
- Team member submitted a report card
- COI expiring within 30 days
- Subscription payment issue

Customer in-app:
- Report card ready
- Walk reminder (24h before)
- Refund issued

Team member in-app:
- New walk assigned
- Walk schedule changed

8.4 Time-based notifications?

- Walk reminder to customer: 24 hours before scheduled start
- COI expiry warning: 30 days, 14 days, 7 days, 1 day before
  expiry
- Subscription past due sequence: day 1, 7, 14, 21, 28, 30
  (per Part 5.4)
- Monthly owner summary: first of the month, for the previous
  month

8.5 Opt-outs — clear by recipient:

OPT-OUT ALLOWED:

- Owner can opt out of: marketing emails (none planned for v1,
  structure built for future), monthly summary emails
- Customer can opt out of: marketing emails (none planned for
  v1), optional walk-complete SMS, in-walk photo SMS
- Team member can opt out of: marketing emails (none planned
  for v1)

MANDATORY, CANNOT OPT OUT:

- Owner receives: magic link login emails, payment/billing
  emails, COI status emails, territory status emails, resale
  transaction emails
- Customer receives: magic link login emails, meet-and-greet
  confirmation emails, walk booking confirmations, report
  card emails, "On My Way" SMS, access problem SMS, refund
  issued emails
- Team member receives: magic link login emails, team invite
  emails, walk assignment emails, removal-from-team emails
- AppStack admin (Bryan) receives: territory purchase approval
  requests, COI approval requests, resale transaction
  completions, platform health alerts

Mandatory items cannot be disabled because they are the product
or required for the account to function.

8.6 Email from address + branded subdomains:

Corporate:
- hello@neighbordogs.com (customer-facing and owner-facing)
- admin@neighbordogs.com (AppStack admin alerts)

Factory rule: every Neighbor* vertical gets its own
hello@neighbor[vertical].com.

Branded subdomains (new in rebuild):
- Each owner gets a personalized subdomain at activation
- Uses slugified business name: "Diane's Dog Walking" →
  dianes-dog-walking.neighbordogs.com
- "Two Dianes" problem solved by business name (not personal
  name)
- Validation: lowercase letters, numbers, hyphens; 3-40
  characters; no leading/trailing hyphen; blocklist (admin,
  api, app, www, mail, help, support, billing, neighbordogs,
  appstack, ~50 others); profanity filter; unique
- Vercel wildcard SSL handles certs automatically
- Cloudflare wildcard DNS

No self-service subdomain changes. AppStack admin handles rare
change requests (rebrand, business name change). Old URL
redirects to new for 12 months so nothing breaks.

Email in v1: use hello@neighbordogs.com with display name set
to owner's business name ("Diane's Dog Walking via
NeighborDogs"). No custom per-owner email addresses in v1 —
DNS complexity not worth it.

Factory-level module: "Branded Subdomain Routing" — reusable
across every Neighbor* vertical.

8.7 Emails in user's language?

Yes. Multilingual infrastructure ported from HostClaim (MOD-040).
6 languages supported (en, de, es, fr, it, pt). Locale stored
on user profile drives email template language.

Markets verified international: Europe had 34M dog walking app
users in 2023 (UK 11.4M, Germany 6.2M, France 5.7M). Australia,
Japan also significant. The 6 AppStack languages cover every
major dog walking market.

v1 launch: infrastructure active, content English-only. Other
5 languages added as expansion happens.

Preferred language function (ported from HostClaim):
- Every user type has preferred language on profile
- Owner: set at territory activation
- Team member: set at invite acceptance
- Customer: set at registration
- AppStack admin: English (Bryan)
- Language picker in app (globe icon, dropdown)
- Change takes effect immediately across all future
  communications
- No forced alignment between owner and customer — each user
  receives content in their own language

8.8 Communication to third parties on user's behalf?

No. Unlike HostClaim, NeighborDogs IS the platform. All
communication is between AppStack, owner, team member, and
customer. No third party.

PART 9 — FILES AND MEDIA
==================================================

Note: Much of the photo storage function ported directly from
HostClaim (MOD-025 File/Photo Storage, MOD-055 Document/Photo

9.1 File upload types?

Photos:
- Customer: dog photos during registration (profile photos,
  optional)
- Owner: in-walk photos (2 per walk per report card), meet-
  and-greet photos (optional), profile photo
- Team member: in-walk photos during their walks

PDFs:
- Owner: general liability insurance certificate (COI) upload
  at activation and renewal

No video uploads in v1.

9.2 Maximum file size?

- Photos: 10MB per image (phone cameras produce 3-8MB JPEGs)
- COI PDFs: 5MB

Client-side compression if phone produces larger file.

9.3 Public or private files?

Photos:
- In-walk photos: shareable via tokenized URL (customer
  gallery pattern). Not guessable, not indexed, viewable by
  anyone with the link. Customers share to social media.

- Webhook delay: state reconciles when webhooks resume

Resend down: queue + retry (HostClaim pattern). Main flow
never blocks on email.

Twilio down: queue + retry. Critical SMS ("On My Way", access
problem) fallback to email. Main flow never blocks on SMS.

Geocoding down: customer registration blocks. Clear error.

Open-Meteo down: booking proceeds without weather, fetched
later if service returns.

Today view (owner home screen) — IN v1:

day execution.

  weather, exceptions)
- Walks in order, one card per walk (time, customer, dog,

  primary button)
- Quick actions (report access problem, emergency walk requests)

- Scheduled → "On My Way"
- En Route → "Start Walk"

- Completed → summary, report card link

Team member Today view: same screen, scoped to only walks
